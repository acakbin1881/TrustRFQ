The problem

On-chain liquidity can't move institutional size

Those are pool prices, and the pools are structurally shallow. At block size an order moves the very price it is quoted against, so the slippage is not a fee you pay once. It is a price change you cause. Past a certain size, clearing a block on a DEX is not expensive. It is arithmetically impossible.

The solution

OTC Desk

01

Zero slippage

Stellar DEX pools are too shallow to absorb institutional size without punishing the price. On the desk, the number you sign is the number that settles.

02

No intent leak

Order books broadcast intent to the whole network. Directed orders on TrustRFQ are visible only to the counterparty you addressed. Front-running has nowhere to run.

03

Atomic settlement

No CEX, no off-chain escrow, no capital leaving Stellar. Both legs of the trade move in a single Soroban transaction, or neither does.

How it works

From agreement to atomic swap, in three steps.

  1. 01

    Agree & sign

    You agree terms with your counterparty and create a directed order — amounts, tokens, expiry, their address. Your wallet signs the exact payload.

  2. 02

    Counter-sign

    The order appears live on their desk. They accept, and each wallet signs a Soroban authorization binding every argument of the fill — amounts included.

  3. 03

    Atomic fill

    Anyone can submit the fill carrying both signatures. Both legs move in one transaction — or nothing moves at all.

Security

Built for size. Verified by the chain.

Soroban-enforced

Signature-bound
