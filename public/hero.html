The problem

On-chain liquidity can't move institutional size

Stellar's deepest XLM/USDC liquidity sits in the single-digit millions. The constant-product model (x · y = k) turns punitive at scale — every block trade pays a structural slippage tax.

That last bar is not a rounding error. The same $1M clears off-chain on Coinbase or Binance OTC for roughly $1,000 — 100× cheaper than the pool.

The solution

RFQ execution, settled on-chain

TrustRFQ decouples price discovery from AMM pools. Terms are agreed peer-to-peer, off-book — then settled as a deterministic atomic swap. Institutional execution, on-chain on Stellar.

0

Zero slippage

Quotes are firm — a fixed amount-in for a fixed amount-out. What the maker signs is exactly what settles. No curve, no impact.

∅

No intent leak

Block size never hits a lit order book. Orders are routed privately to one counterparty, so bots can't front-run and liquidity can't flee.

⇄

Atomic settlement

A Soroban smart contract moves both legs in a single transaction. Either both sides settle or neither does — no counterparty risk.

How it works

From agreement to atomic swap in three steps

  1. 01

    Agree & sign

    You agree terms with your counterparty and create a directed order — amounts, tokens, expiry, their address. Your wallet signs the exact payload.

  2. 02

    Counter-sign

    The order appears live on their desk. They accept, and each wallet signs a Soroban authorization binding every argument of the fill — amounts included.

  3. 03

    Atomic fill

    Anyone can submit the fill carrying both signatures. Both legs move in one transaction — or nothing moves at all.

Security model

The trust lives in the signatures

No accounts, no custodian, no admin switches. Every safeguard is cryptographic — enforced by the Stellar network, not by a middleman.

Wallet = identity
No sign-up, no email, no password. Your Stellar wallet is your identity, and every action you take is a wallet signature.
Tamper-proof terms
Both signatures bind every argument of the on-chain fill. Change a single stroop and both signatures die — the transaction reverts.
Replay-guarded
Each order settles at most once: a per-order guard, per-signature nonces, and a hard expiration — all enforced on-chain.
Non-custodial
The contract never holds funds. Both legs move wallet-to-wallet inside the fill, atomically.
